## Executive Summary

### The Comfortable Lie We Tell Ourselves
Traditional investment analysis assumes continuity - that tomorrow will be a comprehensible iteration of today. We model discount rates, project cash flows, and debate whether the S&P will return 7% or 9% annually. 

This is akin to debating optimal cavalry positions in 1913.

### The Uncomfortable Truth
We are experiencing a technological discontinuity that makes traditional valuation models not just wrong, but semantically meaningless. The advancement from GPT-3 to Claude Opus 4.1 in 24 months represents a rate of capability expansion that breaks our conceptual frameworks. We cannot price what we cannot comprehend, and we cannot comprehend what evolves faster than our ability to model it.

### The Meta-Investment Thesis
Stop trying to predict the unpredictable. Instead, construct anti-fragile positioning that benefits from the volatility of our collective inability to price the future.

## Part II: The Valuation Impossibility

### Traditional Metrics in an AI World

#### P/E Ratios
What earnings multiple is appropriate for a company whose entire workforce might be automated within 5 years? Or one that might capture 90% of its market via AI dominance?

#### DCF Models
How do you discount cash flows when the discount rate assumes stable monetary policy, but AI-driven unemployment might force permanent ZIRP?

#### Book Value
What's the value of industrial plant when AI-designed factories make it obsolete? What's the value of office real estate when knowledge work happens in data centres?

> **The Paradox:** The more precisely we model, the more precisely wrong we become.
